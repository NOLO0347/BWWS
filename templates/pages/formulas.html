Chlorination

North Carolina requires all water wells to be disinfected after construction, repairs, maintenance, or pump installation and testing. The standard method of disinfection is to produce a chlorine concentration of a minimum 100 parts per million (ppm) in the water. To achieve this, most well contractors standardize using Calcium Hypochlorite CIO

  • Stabilized chlorine tablets or hypochlorite products containing fungicides, algaecides, or other disinfectants shall NOT be used.
  • The chlorine solution shall stand in the well for a minimum of 24 hours



Calculating headloss for switch/pump sizing

Reminder: The UPC requires a minimum of 15 PSI output at each fixture.

Household Pressure
SWi − (FH × .433) = Fixture PSI
FH = Foot-Head.
SWi = Switch Cut-in.
.433 = 1 foot of head.
Lift Pressure
LP + HP = FH
HP = (SWo × 2.31)
2.31 = 1 PSI.
FH = Foot-Head.
LP = Lift-Pressure.
SWo = Switch Cut-out.
HP = Household-Pressure.
