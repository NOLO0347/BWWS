Pressure Loss due to Friction and Gravity

The Uniform Plumbing Code requires a minimum of 15 PSI at each fixture.

Here we will cover two forms of pressures:
  1. Household pressure is measured in terms of PSI (pounds per square inch), i.e. the pressure tank operates through a range of 30 to 50 PSI, for example.
  2. Lift pressure is measured in terms of 'feet of head', i.e. the pumping level is 60 feet below the pressure tank.

Just remember you can use either term to express pressure but you must be consistent in your formulas. And also remember you can convert from one to the other by using the factors:
1 ft of head = .433 PSI. or conversly 1 PSI = 2.3 ft of head

Household Pressure

It is the pressure switch cut-in setting (30 PSI for a 30/50 switch) that determines the minimum system pressure at the furthest fixture in the house (ex. the showerhead on the second floor). With a 30/50 pressure switch, the minimum system pressure at the tank will be 30 PSI. From the conversion formula above, for each foot in elevation above the tank, you lose .433 pounds.

Using the formula and examples below, let's calculate which pressure switch we will need for each application.

SWi − (FH × .433) = SWi = Switch Cut-in | FH = Foot Head | .433 = 1 foot of head

Good Example:
Two story home with the pressure tank in the basement, the furthest fixture is the second floor showerhead approximately 23 feet above the tank. You are wanting to install a 30/50 switch.

30 − (23 × .433) =
30 − 9.95 = 20.05 PSI a 30/50 switch meets the required 15psi minimum target.
Bad Example:
Three story home with the pressure tank at the well head and below the grade of the house, the grade is approximately 10 feet. The furthest fixture is the third floor showerhead approximately 31 feet above the ground floor. You are wanting to install a 30/50 switch.

30 − ((31 + 10) × .433) =
30 − (41 × .433) = 17.75
30 − 17.75 = 12.25 PSI a 30/50 switch does NOT meet the required 15psi minimum target.

Revision: How about a 40/60 switch?

40 − ((31 + 10) × .433) =
40 − (41 × .433) = 17.75
40 − 17.75 = 22.25 PSI a 40/60 switch meets the required 15psi minimum target.
Lift Pressure

We have a well where the static water level is 50 feet below the pressure tank, and the well drawdown is 10 feet, making the pumping level 60 feet. The household pressure requirements would be 50 PSI because you are using a 30/50 pressure switch. The total pressure required in terms of feet of head, would be:

Lift pressure: 60 ft.
Household pressure: 50 PSI × 2.31 = 115.5 ft.
Total: 175.5 ft.

and that gives us a total pump pressure requirement of 175.5 feet of head. This is the number you will use to select your pump.

Converting from feet of head back to PSI you get: 175.5 ft. × .433 = 76 PSI.

Mathmatically speaking, it doesn't matter which terms you use, just be consistent. You can not add feet of head to PSI in an equation without converting to a common term because all factors in the equation must be in the same terms.
