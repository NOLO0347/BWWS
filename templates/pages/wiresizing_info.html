Wire sizing when two or more cable sizes are involved

Depending on the installation, any number of combinations of cable may be used.

Example:

A well already has 160 feet of buried #10 cable between the service entrance and the well head. A new 3 HP, 230 volt, single phase motor is being installed to replace a smaller motor. The question is: since there is already 160 feet of #10 AWG installed, what size cable is required in the well with a 3 HP, 230 volt, single phase motor setting at 310 feet? The allowed run for a 3HP pump on #10 wire is 300 feet

160 (A. length) ÷ 300 (M. allowed) = 0.533.

This means 53.3% of the allowable voltage drop or loss, which is allowed between the service entrance and the motor, occurs in this wire. This leaves 46.7% of some other wire size to use in the remaining 310 feet "down hole" wire run.

The table shows #8 AWG copper wire is good for 470 feet. Using the second half of the formula,
310 (A. length) ÷ 470 (M. allowed) = 0.660.

Adding this to the 0.533 determined earlier = 1.193. Since this combination is greater than 1.0, the voltage drop will NOT meet the US NEC recommendations.

The table shows #6 AWG copper wire is good for 750 feet. Using the second half of the formula again,
310 ÷ 750 = 0.413

and using these numbers, 0.533 + 0.413 = .0946 which is less than 1.0 and will meet the NEC recommended voltage drop. This works for two, three and more combinations of wire and it does not matter which size wire comes first in the installation.
